Com Tam

When it comes to Saigon cuisine, people can't help but get excited about the dish called "Com Tam". Although rice is usually a filling dish, Saigon's broken rice can be light and delicate in the hands of the eater every early morning. This type of rice doesn't make people feel overly full, so it can be eaten at almost any time. READ MORE

Hu Tieu

If you are looking for a filling but less greasy drink, you can think of Nam Vang noodle soup. The distinctive feature of Nam Vang noodle soup is that the minced meat is added to the broth. The broth is cooked from bone marrow, dried shrimp, and dried squid, so it is very sweet. READ MORE

Bot Chien

Fried flour is a familiar image to many young people in Saigon. The square-cut flour blocks are crispy on the outside but soft and chewy on the inside. The outer layer is a golden egg with a few strands of shredded green papaya. READ MORE

Pha Lau

Almost everyone living in Saigon has heard of the dish called "Pha Lau". The dish originated from the Teochew people and is so familiar that people can immediately imagine it when mentioned. In fact, Pha Lau is a form of utilizing the internal organs of a pig to cook a complete dish. However, the attraction of Pha Lau lies not only in the dish itself. READ MORE

Goi Cuon

The name of this dish probably originates from its ingredients and the way it is made. In terms of appearance, spring rolls have many similarities to beef rolls. However, spring rolls are still highly regarded for their filling, the way they are prepared to retain the original flavor, and the variety of vegetables served with them. READ MORE

Banh Cong

The specialty of Ninh Kieu wharf that tourists cannot miss is "Banh Cong". "Banh Cong" is golden, crispy, fragrant, and delicious with a rich filling, including mung beans; The meat is marinated just right and the fresh shrimp is golden and crispy on top. READ MORE

Lau Vit

Duck hotpot with fermented tofu is a delicious dish in Can Tho that is highly favored. The duck meat is marinated with fermented tofu and other spices to enhance the flavor, then cooked in a hotpot. The hotpot broth includes straw mushrooms, blood cubes, taro, and other ingredients to add natural sweetness, and of course, it is served with vegetables. READ MORE

Banh Xeo

If you don't know what to eat in Can Tho, then Banh Xeo is a fantastic suggestion. Banh Xeo is a delicious specialty dish from the Mekong Delta region. The pancake has a shiny golden crust that is thin and crispy. The filling is packed with minced meat, shrimp, and bean sprouts. READ MORE

Tam Bi

One of the specialties in Can Tho that leaves a lasting impression on tourists is the Tam Bi. The steaming hot strands of Tam Bi are steamed on a charcoal stove and mixed with a soft, thin layer of pork skin. They are then combined with crushed rice crackers, minced garlic, fried garlic, and other ingredients, creating a truly enticing flavor. READ MORE

Oc Nuong

Those who are fans of snails cannot miss the grilled snails with green pepper when coming to Can Tho. The snails are carefully selected, soaked in rice water to remove all the slime, and then lightly boiled. Each snail is grilled on charcoal, seasoned with spices, especially green pepper, until the water inside the snail boils up. The aroma of grilled snails with green pepper captivates every diner.
